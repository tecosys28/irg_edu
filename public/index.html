Two Blockchain-Registered Instruments

TROT Edu offers two distinct instruments to address different stakeholder needs

Future Tuition Rights (FTRs)

Tradable entitlements that lock today's tuition fee for any future eligible student. Pre-paid, locked-fee entitlement.

  • ✓ Tradable and transferable
  • ✓ Locks current fee for future use
  • ✓ Hedges against fee inflation
  • ✓ Global interchangeability

Sponsorship Units (SUs)

Non-enforceable, non-tradable rights allowing sponsors to fully fund a deserving student's education at zero cost to the student.

  • ✓ Pure philanthropic instrument
  • ✓ 80G + CSR eligible (India)
  • ✓ Merit-based allocation
  • ✓ Full transparency via blockchain

How TROT Edu Works

A step-by-step overview of the FTR and Sponsorship Unit ecosystem

1

Institute Registration

Accredited post-12th institutes register on the TROT Edu platform with their accreditation documents, course offerings, annual intake capacity, and escrow banking details.

2

FTR/SU Generation

Institutes generate FTRs or SUs against their seats. Each unit is minted on a permissioned blockchain with a locked fee amount. Issuance is capped at 600% of annual sanctioned intake.

3

Purchase/Donation

FTRs: Households, corporates, or NRIs purchase FTRs to lock current fees for future use.
SUs: Sponsors donate to receive SUs (80G + CSR eligible). Minimum 70% must go to merit/needy students.

4

Hold, Swap, or Trade

FTRs: Can be held, swapped between institutes, or traded. Global interchangeability allows use at any participating institute worldwide.
SUs: Non-tradable but can nominate a deserving student for sponsorship.

5

Redemption

When a student meets eligibility criteria, the FTR/SU holder presents units for redemption. The student pays only the locked historical fee (for FTRs) or nothing at all (for SUs). Upon redemption, seats are replenished.

6

Perpetual Funding Loop

Every redeemed FTR/SU frees up a seat, allowing the institute to issue a fresh unit. This creates a self-replenishing, perpetual funding mechanism.
