How TROT Edu Works

A step-by-step overview of the FTR and Sponsorship Unit ecosystem

1

Institute Registration

Accredited post-12th institutes (Engineering, Medical, Law, Management, etc.) register on the TROT Edu platform with their accreditation documents, course offerings, annual intake capacity, and escrow banking details.

2

FTR/SU Generation

Institutes generate FTRs or SUs against their seats. Each unit is minted on a permissioned blockchain with a locked fee amount. Issuance is capped at 600% of annual sanctioned intake to prevent oversupply.

3

Purchase/Donation

FTRs: Households, corporates, or NRIs purchase FTRs to lock current fees for future use.
SUs: Sponsors/philanthropists donate to receive SUs as return gifts (80G + CSR eligible). Minimum 70% of SUs must be allocated to merit/needy students.

4

Hold, Swap, or Trade

FTRs: Can be held, swapped between institutes, or traded on the platform. Global interchangeability allows use at any participating institute worldwide.
SUs: Non-tradable but can be used to nominate a deserving student for sponsorship.

5

Redemption

When a student meets eligibility criteria and seeks admission, the FTR/SU holder presents units for redemption. The student pays only the locked historical fee (for FTRs) or nothing at all (for SUs). Upon redemption, seats are replenished for fresh issuance.

6

Perpetual Funding Loop

Every redeemed FTR/SU frees up a seat, allowing the institute to issue a fresh unit. This creates a self-replenishing, perpetual funding mechanism. Institutes receive immediate corpus, students get affordable education, and the cycle continues.

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about TROT Edu

TROT Edu is a blockchain-based platform that introduces Future Tuition Rights (FTRs) and Sponsorship Units (SUs) — two instruments that enable debt-free education funding. FTRs allow you to lock today's tuition fee for future use, while SUs enable sponsors to fully fund deserving students' education. Both are issued by accredited post-12th educational institutes.
FTR (Future Tuition Right): A tradable, locked-fee entitlement that preserves current tuition fees. The student pays the historical locked fee at redemption. FTRs can be traded, swapped between institutes, or held as an inflation hedge.

Sponsorship Unit (SU): A non-tradable, philanthropic instrument. The sponsor pays the full fee upfront; the sponsored student pays nothing. SUs are 80G + CSR eligible and at least 70% must be allocated to merit/needy students.
No. TROT Edu is strictly merit-based and compliant with AICTE/UGC/NBA regulations. Key distinctions: (1) The donor/purchaser has not applied for any admission while donating/purchasing; (2) A unit holder may never seek admission or sponsor anyone; (3) Admission is contingent on eligibility — there is no guarantee or assurance of admission; (4) SUs are offered as return gifts for donations — there is no quid-pro-quo; (5) The institute has no role in subsequent FTR transactions.
No. TROT Edu is not a 'Collective Investment Scheme' under SEBI regulations. No returns or profits are promised. It is not a digital asset or cryptocurrency. The platform is not a payment aggregator. FTRs are locked-fee educational entitlements, not financial instruments promising appreciation.
Institutes offering accredited post-12th programmes (Engineering, Medical, Management, Law, IT Diplomas, Healthcare Technician, Hospitality, Aviation, Arts, Economics, Vocational Certificates) can register on the platform. You'll need to provide accreditation documents, list of courses offered, annual sanctioned intake per course, and escrow banking details. After verification, you can begin issuing FTRs and SUs.
Institutes can issue a maximum of 600% of their annual sanctioned intake. This is calculated as 50% of seats per year × 12 years of future eligibility. For example, if an institute has 100 seats annually, it can issue up to 600 FTRs/SUs total. This cap prevents oversupply and ensures seat availability for redemption.
Sponsorship Units (SUs) are pure donations to charitable educational trusts. In India, sponsors can claim: (1) 80G deduction for donations to registered charitable trusts; (2) CSR expenditure eligibility under Section 135 of Companies Act; (3) 100% deductible expense under Section 37 for FTRs purchased as employee benefits.
The platform provides real-time impact dashboards for sponsors. You can track: number of students sponsored, institutes benefited, courses covered, and overall contribution metrics. Every unit is traceable on the blockchain from sponsor to student to institute, ensuring complete transparency.
This parametric rule ensures that the philanthropic intent of SUs is preserved. A minimum of 70% of issued Sponsorship Units must be allocated to public merit or economically needy students. The remaining 30% may be used by corporates as employee retention benefits. This balance ensures societal impact while allowing corporate participation.
When you purchase an FTR, you lock in the current tuition fee. Even if fees increase by 8-12% annually over the years, your child will only pay the original locked fee at the time of redemption. This protects families from unpredictable fee escalations and enables better financial planning.
Yes, FTRs offer global interchangeability. You can swap or redeem FTRs at any participating institute worldwide — for example, purchase from an Engineering college in Delhi today and redeem at a Medical college in Bangalore or even a Hospitality institute in Singapore later. This flexibility is a key differentiator of TROT Edu.
FTRs do not guarantee admission — they only guarantee fee locking. The student must still meet the institute's and regulatory body's eligibility criteria for admission. If your child doesn't qualify, you can: (1) Trade or sell the FTR on the platform; (2) Swap it for a different course/institute where eligibility is met; (3) Transfer it to another family member; (4) Hold it for future use.
Students can register on the platform and indicate they are looking for sponsorship. SU holders (sponsors, corporates, or philanthropists) can nominate deserving students for sponsorship based on merit and need criteria. Once nominated and the student meets eligibility requirements, the SU is redeemed and the student receives admission with zero fee liability.

TROT Edu uses a permissioned blockchain for all transactions. Every FTR/SU issuance, transfer, swap, and redemption is recorded on an immutable ledger. This creates an end-to-end audit trail traceable from sponsor → student → institute, ensuring complete transparency for all stakeholders and regulators.
The platform implements industry-standard security measures including: encrypted data storage, secure authentication with OTP verification, password requirements (12+ characters with mixed case, numbers, and special characters), and escrow banking for fund management. All sensitive operations require confirmation to prevent accidental actions.
A reserve pool of sponsorship units (approximately 5% of issued units) is maintained in the TROT system's name. If an institute ceases operations, these reserve units can be allotted to affected unit holders, allowing them to use their entitlements at other participating institutes. The global interchangeability of FTRs also provides flexibility to swap to other institutes.
