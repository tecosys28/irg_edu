Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions

What is an FTR? ▼
An FTR (Future Tradable Right) is a Blockchain registered Right to buy certain services or goods at a pre-fixed price any time in the future. It's a way to lock in today's prices for future purchases.
How is FTR different from a gift card or prepaid service? ▼
Unlike gift cards, FTRs are transferable on a marketplace, can be held for multiple years, and represent a binding right backed by blockchain verification. You're not prepaying for a service — you're securing a right at a guaranteed price.
Can I sell my FTR if my plans change? ▼
Yes. FTRs are tradable rights. You can list them on the marketplace and transfer ownership to another buyer. The transfer is recorded on the blockchain.
What if the service provider cannot fulfill my FTR? ▼
Providers maintain a corpus fund to ensure fulfillment. In rare cases of non-fulfillment, you're entitled to surrender your FTR for the surrender value (a percentage of the base price) or seek resolution through our grievance mechanism.
How do I become an FTR provider? ▼
Service providers can apply to mint FTRs by registering on the platform, completing KYC verification, and submitting their capacity details for review. Approved providers can issue FTRs backed by their future service capacity.
How is my FTR secured? ▼
Every FTR uses a 3-layer identification system: a public ID for human reference, an encrypted verification code, and a blockchain hash. This ensures your right is verifiable, tamper-proof, and uniquely yours.
What blockchain does FTR use? ▼
FTRs are registered on a secure, established blockchain network. The platform handles all blockchain interactions — you don't need any technical knowledge to buy, hold, or trade FTRs.
