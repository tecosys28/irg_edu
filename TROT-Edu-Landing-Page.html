Two Powerful Instruments

Blockchain-registered entitlements that lock in pre-fixed educational costs for future eligibility-based admissions.

IRG Edu FTRs

Tradable entitlements that lock today's tuition fee for any future eligible student. Purchase at current fees, redeem later at the same locked price.

  • Tradable and transferable globally
  • Locks current fee for future use
  • Hedges against fee inflation
  • Swap between participating institutes

Sponsorship Units (SUs)

Non-enforceable, non-tradable rights allowing sponsors to fully fund a deserving student's education at zero cost to the student.

  • Pure philanthropic instrument
  • 80G + CSR eligible (India)
  • Merit-based allocation (70% minimum)
  • Full transparency via blockchain

How It Works

A simple, transparent process for all stakeholders

1

Institute Registers

Accredited institutes register with their courses, intake capacity, and escrow banking details.

2

Units Generated

Institutes generate IRG Edu FTRs or SUs against their seats, minted on a permissioned blockchain.

3

Purchase/Donate

Households purchase IRG Edu FTRs to lock fees. Sponsors donate to receive SUs for philanthropy.

4

Redeem at Admission

When eligibility is met, units are redeemed. Student pays locked historical fee or nothing (SU).

Built-in Safeguards

Strict compliance and transparency measures protect all stakeholders

Merit-Based Only

Fully AICTE/UGC/NBA compliant. No capitation fees. Admission based on eligibility.

600% Issuance Cap

Maximum 600% of annual sanctioned intake to prevent oversupply and ensure seat availability.

70% Merit Allocation

Minimum 70% of SUs must be allocated to merit-based or economically needy students.

Blockchain Audit Trail

Every transaction recorded on immutable ledger. End-to-end traceable from sponsor to institute.

Not a Security/CIS

Not a 'Collective Investment Scheme' under SEBI. No returns or profits promised.

Escrow Banking

Secure collection and corpus fund management through regulated escrow accounts.
